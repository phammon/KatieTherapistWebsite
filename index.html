Our Foundation

Healing, Empowerment, and Grace

Healing

Healing is at the heart of the therapy process because it creates the foundation for lasting growth and change. When we experience pain, trauma, loss, or ongoing stress, those experiences can leave emotional wounds. Just like physical wounds, if they aren't cared for, they can continue to hurt, limit, or affect the way we move through life.

Therapy provides a safe, supportive space to tend to those wounds. Healing in therapy doesn't mean forgetting the past or pretending struggles never happened — it means learning how to process emotions, release what no longer serves us, and find healthier ways to cope.

Healing is important because it allows clients to:

  • Reduce emotional suffering and make sense of painful experiences
  • Reconnect with themselves and others in more authentic, meaningful ways
  • Build resilience so they can face future challenges without being overwhelmed
  • Restore hope and confidence, knowing that change is possible

Without healing, therapy would only address the surface. Healing digs deeper, helping clients break free from cycles of self-doubt, fear, or unhealthy patterns. It makes space for empowerment and for the grace to be human — recognizing that while life will always bring difficulties, you don't have to face them alone or without tools.

Empowerment

Empowerment is about helping clients recognize their own strength, voice, and ability to create change in their lives. Many people come to therapy feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or powerless in the face of their emotions, relationships, or circumstances. Empowerment shifts that perspective — reminding clients that while challenges are real, so is their capacity to respond to them.

In the therapy process, empowerment is important because it allows clients to:

  • Take ownership of their healing rather than feeling defined by past struggles
  • Develop confidence in their ability to make decisions and set healthy boundaries
  • Practice skills that build resilience, so they can manage emotions and stress more effectively
  • Reclaim their voice in relationships, family dynamics, or other areas where they may have felt unheard
  • Strengthen self-worth, learning that they are capable, deserving, and enough as they are

Without empowerment, therapy risks becoming something that is "done to" clients instead of something they actively participate in. When clients feel empowered, they become partners in their healing journey, equipped with tools to face challenges not only now but in the future.

Empowerment doesn't mean life becomes easy or perfect — it means clients leave therapy knowing they are capable of handling difficulties with confidence and clarity.

Grace

Grace is about offering kindness, patience, and understanding — especially to ourselves. Many clients enter therapy carrying not only pain from their past but also self-criticism, shame, or the belief that they "should" be doing better. Grace interrupts that cycle. It reminds us that being human means facing struggles, making mistakes, and sometimes feeling overwhelmed — and that this does not take away our worth.

In the therapy process, grace matters because it helps clients:

  • Release self-judgment, replacing harsh inner criticism with compassion
  • Accept their humanity, recognizing that challenges and setbacks are part of growth
  • Practice self-forgiveness, making it easier to learn from experiences instead of getting stuck in regret
  • Reduce perfectionism, creating space to show up authentically without needing to "get it right" all the time
  • Create healthier relationships, as showing grace to ourselves makes it easier to extend it to others

Without grace, clients may continue chasing unrealistic standards or feel defeated by struggles. With grace, therapy becomes a space of gentleness and acceptance — where clients are encouraged to grow, but also reminded that they are enough right now.

Grace is the bridge between healing and empowerment. Healing helps us process the past, empowerment gives us strength for the future, and grace reminds us to be kind to ourselves along the way.

Why DBT?

A cornerstone of my therapeutic approach
"DBT provides practical skills to support healing, empowerment, and grace in everyday life."

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) is at the heart of my work because it provides practical, life-changing tools that help people not only cope but truly thrive. Unlike traditional talk therapy, DBT is highly skills-based, teaching strategies you can use right away in daily life.

A key part of my therapeutic approach is Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT). DBT is highly effective for helping individuals who struggle with intense emotions, unstable relationships, or patterns of self-criticism. I use DBT-based interventions to help clients build the skills they need to live more balanced and fulfilling lives.

In my practice, I focus especially on supporting young adults and women, who often face unique pressures such as identity exploration, relationship challenges, life transitions, and high expectations for performance in school, work, or family roles. DBT is especially powerful for young adults and women, who often find themselves juggling intense emotions, relationship stress, and the pressure to "hold it all together."

What to Expect in Your First Session

A comfortable, judgment-free space to share what's on your mind

Starting therapy can feel a little nerve-wracking, but my goal is to make our first session a comfortable, judgment-free space where you can share what's on your mind at your own pace.

We'll start by getting to know each other. I'll ask about what brought you to therapy, what's been feeling difficult lately, and what you hope to gain from our work together. If you're not sure where to start, that's completely okay—I'll help guide the conversation.

We'll also talk about:

  • Your past experiences with therapy (if any) and what has or hasn't worked for you
  • Any challenges you're facing—whether it's anxiety, relationships, emotional ups and downs, or something else
  • Your strengths and coping skills, so we can build on what's already working
  • Your goals—whether they're big, small, or still unclear, we'll explore what feeling better looks like for you

The first session is also a chance for you to get a feel for how I work and see if we're a good fit. Therapy is a collaborative process, and I want you to feel safe and supported. By the end of our session, we'll discuss next steps, and if you decide to continue, we'll start creating a plan that feels right for you.

The biggest strengths that I bring into our sessions:

Unconditional Positive Regard, Genuineness and Empathetic Understanding. I want to be on your team and help you reach your goals!
