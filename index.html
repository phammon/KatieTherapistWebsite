Why DBT Works

DBT provides practical skills to support healing, empowerment, and grace in everyday life

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) is at the heart of my work because it provides practical, life-changing tools that help people not only cope but truly thrive. Unlike traditional talk therapy, DBT is highly skills-based, teaching strategies you can use right away in daily life.

🧘‍♀️ Mindfulness

Stay grounded in the present moment, slow down racing thoughts, and feel calmer when overwhelmed.

❤️ Emotion Regulation

Learn to identify, tolerate, and manage overwhelming feelings without shutting down or becoming reactive.

🌊 Distress Tolerance

Use skills like opposite action to break free from cycles of avoidance or unhealthy coping strategies.

🤝 Interpersonal Effectiveness

Practice setting boundaries, communicating needs, and fostering healthier connections with others.

Through this approach, I help clients develop confidence in their ability to handle life's challenges while giving themselves grace along the way. My goal is to empower women and young adults to not only manage their mental health but to thrive with a sense of balance, resilience, and hope.

Perinatal Mental Health Support

Specialized Care for Mothers During Pregnancy & Postpartum

I hold a Perinatal Mental Health Certification (PMH-C), which allows me to provide specialized care for women during pregnancy and the postpartum period.

Motherhood brings joy and connection, but it can also be overwhelming and isolating. Many women experience anxiety, depression, intrusive thoughts, or challenges adjusting to life changes before and after birth.

This is a season of life that deserves grace, support, and healing. I am honored to walk with mothers through their journeys, helping them feel seen, understood, and empowered.

With my training and experience, I help moms:

  • Navigate the emotional ups and downs of pregnancy and postpartum
  • Manage perinatal anxiety, depression, or OCD symptoms with compassion and evidence-based care
  • Build confidence in themselves as mothers while maintaining a sense of personal identity
  • Strengthen coping skills to balance the demands of family, relationships, and self-care

What to Expect in Your First Session

A comfortable, judgment-free space to share what's on your mind

Starting therapy can feel a little nerve-wracking, but my goal is to make our first session comfortable where you can share what's on your mind at your own pace. We'll start by getting to know each other, and I'll ask about what brought you to therapy, what's been feeling difficult lately, and what you hope to gain from our work together.

We'll also talk about:

  • Your past experiences with therapy (if any) and what has or hasn't worked for you
  • Any challenges you're facing—whether it's anxiety, relationships, emotional ups and downs, or something else
  • Your strengths and coping skills, so we can build on what's already working
  • Your goals—whether they're big, small, or still unclear, we'll explore what feeling better looks like for you

The first session is also a chance for you to get a feel for how I work and see if we're a good fit. Therapy is a collaborative process, and I want you to feel safe and supported. By the end of our session, we'll discuss next steps, and if you decide to continue, we'll start creating a plan that feels right for you.

The biggest strengths that I bring into our sessions:

Unconditional Positive Regard, Genuineness and Empathetic Understanding. I want to be on your team and help you reach your goals!
